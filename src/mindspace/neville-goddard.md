# Neville Goddard

**🎭 The Imagination Man: Creating Reality Through Consciousness**

Neville Goddard (1905-1972) was a mystic, author, and lecturer who taught one of the most powerful and direct paths to conscious creation: **the law of assumption**. His teaching is deceptively simple yet profoundly deep—what you assume to be true, you will experience in your world.

Originally from Barbados, Neville began his spiritual journey as a theater actor before experiencing a profound mystical awakening. He studied under Abdullah, an Ethiopian rabbi who introduced him to esoteric interpretations of Scripture, which became the foundation of his unique teaching style.

---

## Core Teaching: The Law of Assumption

At the heart of Neville's message is a single, revolutionary principle:

> **"Assume the feeling of your wish fulfilled and your desire will come to pass."**

This isn't wishful thinking or positive affirmation—it's the deliberate use of imagination to create reality. Neville taught that **imagination is God**, and when you use imagination consciously, you're operating as the creator you were born to be.

### The Four Steps of Creation

1. **Define Your Desire** - Know exactly what you want
2. **Construct a Scene** - Create a short scene that implies it's fulfilled
3. **Live in the End** - Repeatedly experience the feeling of it being done
4. **Persist** - Remain faithful to your assumption regardless of appearances

## Key Concepts

### 1. The Human Imagination IS God

Neville's most controversial and profound teaching: **your imagination is not a tool of God—it is God itself**. When you imagine, you're operating as the divine creator.

> "God is your imagination. The creative power of God and your imagination are one and the same."

### 2. The Promise (Bible Interpretation)

Neville taught that the Bible is not historical but psychological—a dramatic story of human consciousness. Biblical stories are instructions for how to use your imagination:

- **Moses** represents the law (assumption)
- **Jesus** represents your human imagination
- **Christ** represents the divine imagination awakened in you
- **The Promised Land** represents the state you wish to embody

### 3. States of Consciousness

You are always living in a "state"—a collection of beliefs, feelings, and assumptions. To change your life, you must change your state:

- **Poverty consciousness** creates lack
- **Health consciousness** creates vitality  
- **Wealth consciousness** creates abundance
- **Love consciousness** creates fulfilling relationships

### 4. Revision

One of Neville's most powerful techniques: **mentally rewrite past events**. By revising how you remember past experiences, you change their effect on your present and future.

## Essential Books and Their Messages

### *The Power of Awareness* (1952)

**The Foundation: Understanding Conscious Creation**

This is Neville's most accessible and comprehensive introduction to his core teachings. It lays out the fundamental principles of conscious creation and the nature of reality.

**Key teachings:**
- The difference between facts and creative power
- How to distinguish between conscious and subconscious creation
- The importance of living from the end
- Why assumptions must be felt, not just thought

### *Feeling is the Secret* (1944)

**The Technique: Mastering Emotional Creation**

A short but potent work focusing on the emotional component of creation. Neville explains that **feeling is the secret** to making imaginal acts effective.

**Key teachings:**
- Why feeling, not thought, is the creative factor
- How to generate the feeling of the wish fulfilled
- The relationship between feeling and belief
- Why emotional intensity matters more than repetition

### *Awakened Imagination* (1954)

**The Mystery: Biblical Interpretation and Advanced Teachings**

This book reveals Neville's esoteric interpretation of Scripture and introduces more advanced concepts about the nature of reality and consciousness.

**Key teachings:**
- The Bible as psychological drama
- The meaning of biblical stories and characters
- The concept of the "real belief"
- How to recognize when you're truly creating

### *The Law and the Promise* (1961)

**The Integration: Bringing It All Together**

Written near the end of his life, this work synthesizes Neville's complete teaching with personal stories of miraculous manifestations.

**Key teachings:**
- Real-life examples of the law in action
- How to handle doubt and conflicting appearances
- The relationship between the law (assumption) and the promise (Scripture)
- Advanced applications for health, wealth, and relationships

## Neville's Creation Techniques

### 1. The Sabbath (SLEEP Technique)

At bedtime, when your mind is relaxed and receptive, **assume the feeling of your wish fulfilled** as you drift off to sleep. This bypasses conscious resistance and plants the seed directly in your subconscious.

### 2. Lullaby Method

Throughout the day, **mentally repeat a short phrase that implies your desire is fulfilled**. Like a lullaby, it soothes the conscious mind and impresses the subconscious.

### 3. Thank You Technique

Act as if your desire has already manifested and **mentally say "thank you"** to whatever force you believe brought it about. This generates genuine gratitude and reinforces the assumption.

### 4. Revision Before Sleep

Review your day and **mentally revise any unwanted events** to match your ideal. This transforms the day's energy and sets up better experiences for tomorrow.

## Why Neville Matters Today

In an age of complexity and overwhelm, Neville offers elegant simplicity:

- **Direct Power** - No intermediaries between you and creation
- **Simplicity** - One principle that works for everything
- **Speed** - Results can manifest quickly when properly applied
- **Universality** - The same law works for health, wealth, relationships, anything

Neville strips away spiritual complexity and reveals the fundamental creative mechanism of consciousness itself.

## How to Practice Neville's Teachings

1. **Choose one desire** - Don't scatter your energy
2. **Create a short scene** - Something simple that implies fulfillment
3. **Feel it real** - Focus on the feeling, not the visualization
4. **Practice at night** - Use the sleepy state for maximum impact
5. **Persist in your assumption** - Ignore contradictory appearances
