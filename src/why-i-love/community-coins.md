Bitcoin is global. That's good.  
But **global doesn't mean human**.

Humans live within **borders**, **languages**, and **laws**.  
We need blockchains that speak to **local problems** with **local solutions**.

That’s where **Community Coins** come in — I call them **Rooty Coins**.

---

### 🌱 Rooty = Grassroots + Sovereign

Coins like:

- **Canada eCoin**
- **eGulden (Netherlands)**
- **Auroracoin (Iceland)**

…aren’t trying to be the next global reserve asset.  
They’re trying to **build self-determination from the ground up**.

They are:

- Sovereign testbeds  
- Locally relevant  
- Politically disruptive  
- Human scale

---

### Why Rooty Coins Matter

> Bitcoin = coordination at global scale  
> Rooty Coins = coordination at human scale

Community coins offer:

- **Localized monetary systems**  
- **On-chain democratic tooling**  
- **Identity integration** (citizen chains, ID overlays)  
- **Cultural relevance** (language, law, symbolism)

Where Bitcoin is neutral and universal,  
**Rooty Coins are opinionated and grounded**.

---

### 🇨🇦 Canada eCoin – My Favorite Rooty Chain

Canada eCoin (CDN) has:

- Fast block times  
- Active developer tools (eCoinCore)  
- Zero Premine / Fair Release

It lets me treat CDN like a **sovereign data bus** for P2P Canadian infra.

---

### Why These Chains Matter for Builders

When I build with Rooty chains:

- I don’t need Wall Street  
- I don’t need venture capital  
- I don’t need permission  
- I don’t need a giant userbase

I need **one village**, **one group of devs**, **one experiment**.

And that’s enough.

---

### Community Coins Are Evolution Engines

Global chains will stabilize.  
**Local chains will mutate**.

And that’s the whole point.  
That’s the lab — the dojo — the sandbox for change.

---

> Rooty chains are the most important class of cryptocurrencies.  
> They serve people, not platforms.  
> They give communities the power to evolve.

---

## In My Stack

I use Rooty coins:

- To experiment with on-chain voting  
- As economic inputs for localized apps  
- For anchoring peer IDs and metadata  
- As **runtime context** inside `koad:io`

They’re not memes.  
They’re **neighborhood superpowers**.

---

**Don’t underestimate the small chains.**  
They’re where the future gets prototyped.
