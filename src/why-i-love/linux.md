> If you can’t read the code, you can’t trust the system.

Linux is **free** as in freedom.

Not just “no cost” — but **no leash**.  
It’s code written by people, for people.  
Millions of contributors. No king.

---

### Community Is the Compiler

- Anyone can audit it
- Anyone can fork it
- Anyone can fix it

There are no backdoors, no permissions needed to patch or fork.  
Just you, the source, and the keyboard.

---

### Anything Less Is a Lie

Proprietary OSes are black boxes.  
You trust what you’re told.

Linux? It shows you *everything*.  
That’s the only foundation real sovereignty can be built on.

---

### Final Thought

> Linux is not an operating system.  
> It’s a *philosophy of freedom*.

If you care about ownership —  
of your machine, your data, your time —  
you’ll find your home in Linux.
