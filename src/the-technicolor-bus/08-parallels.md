# **Parallels**

### **Taking What Resonates**

In your journey of self-discovery and transformation, you will encounter numerous teachers—each offering unique wisdom that can help you unlock different levels of your mind and understanding. These teachings, while sometimes varying in language and approach, often overlap in profound ways. These **parallels** in teachings provide us with a rich tapestry of tools to work with, but it’s important to remember: **take only what resonates with you** and leave the rest.

### **Resonance is Key**

Not every teacher will speak to you at every stage of your journey. Just as your mind evolves, so does your relationship with different teachings. At one point, you may find deep resonance with the words of one teacher, but later, as you evolve and grow, another teacher may suddenly feel more aligned with your current state of awareness. 

**Start where it feels good**. There is no need to force yourself to understand or agree with teachings that don’t resonate with you at the moment. Every teacher is offering a perspective that may be a piece of the puzzle you’re working to solve, but you are the one who must decide which pieces fit best in your life. Allow the journey to unfold naturally and trust that the right lessons will come at the right time.

### **Parallels in Teachings**

There are many influential teachers who offer wisdom that can help illuminate different aspects of your mind and soul. Their teachings may sound different, or even contradictory at times, but underneath it all lies the same universal truth: the power of **mind**, the importance of **imagination**, and the creative force that flows through each of us. Below are just a few key teachers and their contributions that can enhance your journey.

**Joseph Murphy's "The Power of Your Subconscious Mind"**  
This book offers profound insights into how the subconscious mind influences reality. Murphy teaches how we can **reprogram** our subconscious by using positive affirmations and visualization techniques. His work aligns perfectly with the idea of disciplining the 12 disciples of mind and reprogramming outdated belief systems.

**Eckhart Tolle - "The Power of Now"**  
Tolle's teaching focuses on the importance of **presence** and **awareness**. He encourages us to let go of the past and future, and to be fully present in the moment. This teaching aligns with the practice of tuning into the present moment and cultivating mindfulness as part of your mental discipline.

**Dr. Hu Lin - Ho'oponopono**  
Ho'oponopono is a Hawaiian practice of reconciliation and forgiveness. It teaches us to **apologize, forgive, love, and express gratitude**—powerful practices for working with the inner child and the 12 disciples. This can be incorporated to clear negative thought patterns, heal emotional wounds, and restore peace to the mind.

**Abraham Hicks - The Art of Allowing**  
Abraham Hicks teaches us to **allow** the flow of well-being into our lives. By aligning with our inner source and adopting the practice of **allowing**, we invite abundance, health, and joy into our experience. This idea complements the work of quieting the monkey mind and letting the inner child relax and trust the pilot.

**Neville Goddard - Power of Imagination**  
Goddard’s teachings emphasize the creative power of **imagination**. He teaches that by vividly imagining a desired outcome, we can bring it into reality. This aligns perfectly with the concept of the bus and its passengers—where imagination becomes the driving force in shaping our reality.

**Dr. Joe Dispenza - Quantum Mind Model for Healing**  
Dispenza explores the intersection of **mind** and **body** through the lens of quantum physics. He teaches that by changing our thoughts, we can literally change our biological state and **heal** ourselves. His work on rewiring the brain and creating new neural pathways is essential for anyone seeking to elevate their mind and consciousness.

**Carl Jung - Shadow Work**  
Jung’s work on the **shadow**—the parts of ourselves we repress or deny—is crucial for personal transformation. By doing shadow work, we integrate these suppressed aspects of our psyche, ultimately leading to a more whole and authentic self.

**The Kybalion by The Three Initiates**  
The Kybalion distills the **Hermetic principles** into timeless truths that govern reality. These principles, such as **vibration, polarity, and correspondence**, are deeply aligned with the metaphysical laws that govern the mind and spirit. Studying the Kybalion offers an understanding of how the mental, emotional, and physical worlds are interconnected.

**The Gospel According to Jesus (Stephen Mitchell)**  
In Stephen Mitchell’s translation of the Gospel, he highlights the **mystical** teachings of Jesus, emphasizing the power of **love** and the importance of **inner transformation**. Jesus embodies the qualities of the highest truth and serves as a model for the pilot we place in our bus.

**Edgar Cayce**  
Edgar Cayce’s readings touch on many topics, including **health, soul purpose**, and the nature of the **universe**. His work blends spirituality and metaphysics, providing insight into how to live a fulfilling life and understand the deeper forces at play in our lives.

**A Course in Miracles**  
This course offers profound teachings on the power of **forgiveness** and the importance of **choosing love over fear**. It teaches us how to see beyond the illusions of the world and into the eternal truth of our being.

**Norman Vincent Peale**  
Peale's teachings on the **power of positive thinking** emphasize how our thoughts can shape our reality. His work is a great complement to Joseph Murphy’s teachings and provides practical tools for creating a positive mindset.

**Tao Te Ching (Lao Tzu)**  
The Tao Te Ching emphasizes the importance of **balance**, **simplicity**, and **flow** in life. It teaches us to embrace the natural rhythm of the universe and to act from a place of **stillness** and inner peace.

**Bhagavad Gita**  
The Bhagavad Gita, a sacred text of Hinduism, provides a roadmap for living a life of **dharma** (righteous action), **devotion**, and **knowledge**. It emphasizes the connection between the material world and the divine, offering guidance on how to maintain balance amidst life’s challenges.

**The Bible**  
The Bible holds timeless wisdom for spiritual growth, offering profound teachings on **love**, **forgiveness**, and **faith**. Whether you are Christian or not, the Bible’s allegorical teachings provide guidance on the mind's role in shaping our experiences.

**Manly P. Hall**  
Manly P. Hall’s work on **esoteric knowledge** and the deeper mysteries of life provides insight into the hidden forces at play in the universe. His teachings complement the Hermetic principles and the laws of the universe, offering a deeper understanding of the metaphysical world.

**Emmet Fox**  
Emmet Fox’s writings focus on the power of the **mind** to shape our reality. His work emphasizes the importance of positive thought and the power of mental **affirmations** to bring about change.

**Christian D. Larson**  
Larson’s teachings emphasize the power of **thought** and **attitude** in creating our reality. His focus on **self-reliance** and mental discipline aligns with the idea of working with the mind to shape a better life.

**Joel S. Goldsmith**  
Goldsmith’s work focuses on the **infinite presence** of God within us and the transformative power of recognizing this divine connection. His teachings are a reminder of the **interconnectedness** of all things.

**C.S. Lewis**  
C.S. Lewis, known for his spiritual allegories, explores themes of **faith**, **redemption**, and **inner growth**. His work provides profound insights into the journey of spiritual transformation.

**Shakespeare**  
The works of Shakespeare explore the **human condition**, the complexities of the mind, and the eternal dance between light and dark. His writings offer timeless wisdom on how to navigate life’s challenges.

**Florence Scovel Shinn**  
Shinn’s teachings emphasize the power of **words**, **affirmations**, and **mental visualization** to create positive change. She provides practical tools for applying metaphysical principles to everyday life.

## **The Art of Selective Learning**

As you move forward on your journey, remember that **you are the student**, and the world is filled with teachers who offer valuable insights. It is your job to selectively learn from each of them—taking the parts that resonate with you and leaving the rest behind.

Allow your **internal compass**—the pilot and your connection to the divine truth—to guide you in choosing what teachings feel true for you at any given time. You do not need to accept everything a teacher says; take the parts that elevate your consciousness and help you grow.

Ultimately, the truth is within you. As you continue to study, reflect, and grow, you will find the teachings that resonate most deeply and help you build the life you’ve always dreamed of.
